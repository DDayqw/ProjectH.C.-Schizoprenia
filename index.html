SCHIZOPHRENIA

What is it?

Schizophrenia is a chronic mental disorder
affecting more than 21 million people worldwide.
Patients suffer a distortion of reality.
Often hearing voices & experiencing hallucinations


Signs of Schizophrenia

  • Hallucinations

    Victims may see or hear things that don't exist

  • Erratic speech

    Victims may speak in a disorganised and unintelligible manner.

  • Depression

    Increased vulnerability to depression and anxiety.

Treatment

While there is no true treatment, the best way forward is to reduce schizophrenic symptoms

Antipsychotic medication

Antipsychotic medications are the most effective treatment for schizophrenia.
Medications such as Risperdal and Zyprexa have been shown to reduce both the
positive and negative symptoms of schizophrenia by up to 40%.

Psychosocial Therapy

This includes cognitive behavioural therapy, behavioural skills training,
supported employment, and cognitive remediation interventions.

Family education and support

Learn coping mechanisms to better manage their distress, as well as to remain
empathetic and prepared as possible, allowing them the capacity to aid their
family/friend suffering from schizophrenia
